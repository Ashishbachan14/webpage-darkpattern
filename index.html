What are Dark Patterns?

Dark Pattern is a fraudulent technique used in user interfaces to control or trick users into doing things they do not choose to do. This model uses psychological models to encourage behavior that benefits the company or website at the expense of authenticity and choice for the user.

TYPES OF DARK PATTERN

Forced Contunity

"Forced Continuity, a dark pattern, traps users into ongoing payments by making it intentionally difficult to cancel subscriptions. It exploits confusion and inconvenience, leading to users unintentionally and continuously paying for services they may no longer want or need."

Countdown

"Countdown, creates urgency by imposing time limits on users, pressuring them to make rushed decisions. It exploits psychological triggers, encouraging impulsive actions, often leading to unintended purchases or commitments driven by the fear of missing out."

Social Proof

"Social Proof, a sneaky tactic, tricks you by showing fake popularity signals like fake reviews or fake user counts, making you trust a product or service more than you should, leading to potentially bad choices."
